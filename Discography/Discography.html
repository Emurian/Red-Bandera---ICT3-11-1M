Masterpiece

A Night at the opera (1975)


"A Night at the Opera" is a classic rock album released by the British band Queen in 1975. A rock album that illustrates Queen’s wide range of music genre, that includes; rock, opera and ballad. The album includes the band’s masterpiece, titled “Bohemian Rhapsody,” the album also includes these honorable mentions; “You’re My Best Friend” by John Deacon & "Love of My Life" by Brian May.



Jazz (1978)

“Jazz” is the seventh album by the rock band, Queen. Was released in 1978, with a more complex and theatrical style from their style. One of the most popular songs in this release is “Don’t Stop Me Now,” with its upbeat and energetic style, that fully corresponds the band’s style. Some of the notable songs on the album include "Bicycle Race," and "Fat Bottomed Girls.”



The Game (1980)

"The Game" is the eighth studio album by the rock band, released in 1980. Incorporates the essence of rock, pop and dance music. The powerful releases include: "Another One Bites the Dust," "Crazy Little Thing Called Love," and "Play the Game."



The Works (1984)

"The Works" is the eleventh studio album by the British rock band Queen, released in 1984. The album is known for its mix of rock, pop, and electronic elements. The album is constructed by the songs: “Radio Ga Ga,” “I Want to Break Free,” and “Hammer to Fall.” This time, the band members had a little bit of dispute due to their own ideals and own musical vision.



Made in Heaven(1995)

The album "Made in Heaven" by the rock band Queen, released in 1995. This album was deeply showing their love and affection for the band’s lead vocalist, Freddie Mercury, who sadly passed away due to a disease. The album features songs like "Made in Heaven," "I Was Born to Love You," "Too Much Love Will Kill You," "Let Me Live," and many more.
